Important convention: the top-level `objective`, `G`, `P`, and `verification` block usually describe the reported route solution. They do not by themselves prove that the method optimized the original problem. The meaning of `lower_bound`, `upper_bound`, and `gap` is method-specific; in diagnostic methods those fields may be fixed-cap surrogate bounds, pricing costs, or restricted-pool bounds.

## Practical Reporting Rules

Use these rules when reading current result artifacts:

1. `verification.feasible=true` and `verification.objective_matches=true` prove only that the reported routes are feasible and that the reported `objective/G/P` are recomputed correctly.
2. For global original-problem optimality, also require method-scope closure:
   - compact CPLEX/tailored fallback: CPLEX optimal status and `gap=0`;
   - full BPC/frontier: all Gini intervals certified and aggregate LB reaches incumbent;
   - full route-load enumeration: complete enumeration plus exact master.
3. `gap=0` in `gcap-cg`, `gcap-tree`, `master`, `cg`, `pricing`, `cuts`, or `branching` is not a global original-problem gap unless the certificate text explicitly says the original problem was closed.
4. `columns > 0` does not imply a restricted-pool weakness if exact pricing has closed every relevant branch-price node. It does imply a pool-limited certificate for `master` unless the pool is explicitly the full feasible route-load set.
5. A CPLEX incumbent equal to the tailored incumbent is not a certificate unless CPLEX also closes its lower bound.
